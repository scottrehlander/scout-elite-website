After a game has been tracked with Scout Elite Live, Scout Elite gives you rich reports and analytics to understand what happened and why.

## Accessing Game Reports

1. Navigate to **Scout Elite Live** in the sidebar
2. Click on a published game
3. You'll see three tabs: **Report**, **Analytics**, and **Log**

## Report Tab

The Report tab gives you a quick summary of the game:

- **Team Statistics** — a side-by-side comparison of key stats (shots, goals, penalties, faceoffs, etc.)
- **Scoring Log** — chronological list of goals with details
- **Player Statistics** — individual stat breakdowns for each player tracked

This is the best view for a quick post-game debrief or sharing with parents who want to know what happened.

## Analytics Tab

The Analytics tab provides interactive line charts showing how events unfolded over the course of the game.

- **Time-series charts** — see event frequency or cumulative counts plotted against game time
- **Filter by event type** — select which events to display (shots, turnovers, zone entries, etc.)
- **Filter by team** — compare your team's activity against the opponent
- **Identify patterns** — spot momentum swings, pressure periods, and turning points

> **Tip:** Look for stretches where your team had a high volume of shots but no goals — these can reveal finishing problems or identify dominant periods worth reviewing on video.

## Log Tab

The Log tab shows every event recorded during the game in a filterable table:

- **Filter by event type, team, or player**
- **Edit events inline** — fix timestamps, correct player assignments, or add notes
- **Use as a reference** when creating clips — knowing the exact game time of key events speeds up your clipping workflow

## Analysis Hub

For deeper statistical analysis across multiple games, check out the **Analysis Hub** in the sidebar. It provides time-series charts and trend analysis using data from all your published games.
